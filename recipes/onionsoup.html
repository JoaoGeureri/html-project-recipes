French Onion Soup

About French Onion Soup

Although this particular kind of soup has existed since Ancient Rome, it reached its current state during the 18th century. It is said that, after a long, failed hunting trip, Louis XV and his friends came back to the country house to look for something to eat. All they found within the cupboards was some onions, some champagne, and a bit of butter. Since they only had these three ingredients, they decided to throw it all into a pot and cook it. Voila, the French onion soup was thus born.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cups sliced onions
  • 4 (10.5 ounce) cans beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons dry sherry
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 pinch salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 slices French bread
  • 4 slices provolone cheese
  • 2 slices Swiss cheese, diced
  • ¼ cup grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions

  1. Melt butter with olive oil in an 8 quart stock pot on medium heat. Add onions and continually stir until tender and translucent. Do not brown the onions.
  2. Add beef broth, sherry and thyme. Season with salt and pepper, and simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Heat the oven broiler
  4. Ladle soup into oven safe serving bowls and place one slice of bread on top of each (bread may be broken into pieces if you prefer).
  5. Layer each slice of bread with a slice of provolone, 1/2 slice diced Swiss and 1 tablespoon Parmesan cheese.
  6. Place bowls on cookie sheet and broil in the preheated oven until cheese bubbles and browns slightly.
